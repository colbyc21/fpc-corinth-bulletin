Welcome

We Are Glad You Are Here!

We are honored that you decided to join us this morning. Whether it is your first time in church, your first time in a long while, or part of your regular rhythm, we hope you will be encouraged by the fellowship with each other, and with our Lord, as we worship him together.

If this is your first time with us, please take a moment and fill out a Next Steps Card found on the back of the pew. We would love to meet you and help you get connected.

Children & Nursery

Children’s Church is on a short break—the Youth Group will resume leading Children’s Church on June 1st. Nursery rooms for infants through age 3 are in the Education Wing, with the entrance behind the playground on the west side of campus. Children are always welcome in worship!

Our Vision & Mission

Our vision is to see lives transformed by the power of the gospel, rooted in God’s Word and the Reformed tradition, in order to create a culture of discipleship at FPC where disciples WORSHIP holistically, GROW relationally in Christ, and SERVE boldly in the world for the glory of God. Our mission is to accomplish this by “Being Disciples and Making Disciples.”

Sermon Outline

“False Grace and Certain Judgment”

Jude 5–16 • Rev. Waring Porter

One of the greatest dangers facing the church today is not outright atheism or open hostility to Christianity. It is a distorted version of Christianity that speaks often about grace but very little about holiness, repentance, obedience, or the fear of God. Dietrich Bonhoeffer famously called this “cheap grace.”

Today we see forms of Christianity that want the benefits of Jesus without the lordship of Jesus. We see a version of faith that avoids hard truths because it fears offending people more than dishonoring God. Jude reminds us that the holiness of God matters. Truth matters. Doctrine matters. The way we live matters. Because false teaching is never merely intellectual, it is deeply spiritual and profoundly moral. What we believe about grace will shape the kind of disciples we become.

I. Remember God’s Past Judgments (vv. 5–7)

II. The Character of False Teachers (vv. 8–13)

III. The Certainty of Final Judgment (vv. 14–16)

Resources: The Cost of Discipleship, Dietrich Bonhoeffer; Calvin’s Commentaries (Vol. XXII), John Calvin; Peter & Jude, Simon Kistemaker; Matthew Henry’s Commentary (Vol. 6).
