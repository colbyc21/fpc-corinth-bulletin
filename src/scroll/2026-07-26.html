Our Vision & Mission

Our vision is to see lives transformed by the power of the gospel, rooted in God’s Word and the Reformed tradition, in order to create a culture of discipleship at FPC where disciples WORSHIP holistically, GROW relationally in Christ, and SERVE boldly in the world for the glory of God. Our mission is to accomplish this by “Being Disciples and Making Disciples.”

*The Hymn:  #26 “A Mighty Fortress Is Our God” (vv. 1–3)

Hymn “A Mighty Fortress Is Our God” was written by Martin Luther around 1529 and is often called the Battle Hymn of the Reformation. Inspired by Psalm 46, it proclaims God’s sovereign protection and celebrates the unshakable security of God’s people. Even when the world rages, “the Prince of Darkness grim” cannot prevail against Christ and His Church.

“Restored by Grace: Dealing with Opposition”

Rev. Waring Porter  ·  Ezra 4

God’s people will have seasons in life that test them. There are moments when we feel inadequate, discouraged, and completely dependent upon the Lord. In these times, there is a truth that remains crystal clear: anything that God builds will face opposition. Wherever God is building something for His glory, someone, or something, is working to discourage it, distract it, or derail it. Sometimes we assume that if we’re in the center of God’s will, life ought to become easier. Ezra tells us something different. Sometimes the greatest opposition comes precisely because we are in the center of God’s will. That’s exactly where God’s people find themselves in Ezra 4. This chapter asks a question every believer eventually has to answer: How do God’s people respond when God’s work encounters opposition?

I. We Respond with Discernment (vv. 1–5)

“The Lord is my light and my salvation; whom shall I fear?” — Psalm 27:1

II. We Respond with Perseverance (vv. 6–23)

“Let us not grow weary of doing good, for in due season we will reap, if we do not give up.” — Galatians 6:9

“God is our refuge and strength, a very present help in trouble… The Lord of hosts is with us; the God of Jacob is our fortress.” — Psalm 46:1, 11

III. We Respond with Confidence (v. 24)

“Wait for the Lord; be strong, and let your heart take courage; wait for the Lord!” — Psalm 27:14
