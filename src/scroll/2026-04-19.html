Welcome

We Are Glad You Are Here!

We are honored that you decided to join us this morning. Whether it is your first time in church, your first time in a long while, or part of your regular rhythm, we hope you will be encouraged by the fellowship with each other, and with our Lord, as we worship him together.

If this is your first time with us, please take a moment and fill out a Next Steps Card found on the back of the pew. We would love to meet you and help you get connected.

Children & Nursery

Children’s Church — Children ages 4 through 5th grade are welcome in the Fellowship Hall, located at the northeast corner of the church campus. Nursery rooms for infants through age 3 are in the Education Wing, with the entrance behind the playground on the west side of campus. Children are always welcome in worship!

Our Vision & Mission

Our vision is to see lives transformed by the power of the gospel, rooted in God’s Word and the Reformed tradition, in order to create a culture of discipleship at FPC where disciples WORSHIP holistically, GROW relationally in Christ, and SERVE boldly in the world for the glory of God. Our mission is to accomplish this by “Being Disciples and Making Disciples.”

Sermon Outline

“Praying as Christ Taught Us: Lead Us Not into Temptation”

1 Corinthians 10:12–13; Matthew 6:13a • Rev. Waring Porter

In this series, we have seen how the Lord’s Prayer shapes every aspect of the Christian life: our worship, our dependence, and our repentance. Now we turn to the final petitions, where Jesus teaches us to seek God’s ongoing protection and preservation. In this next petition, “Lead us not into temptation,” we are reminded that the Christian life is not lived in our own strength, but in daily reliance on the faithfulness of our God. This petition humbles us by exposing our weakness, while at the same time comforting us with the assurance that our heavenly Father is our Keeper, who guards us, strengthens us, and preserves us in the midst of every trial.

I.  The Reality of Temptation

James 1:13 — “Let no one say when he is tempted, ‘I am being tempted by God,’ for God cannot be tempted with evil, and he himself tempts no one.”

II.  The Faithfulness of God

Psalm 121:3, 5 — “He will not let your foot be moved; he who keeps you will not slumber…The Lord is your keeper.”

Hebrews 4:15 — “For we do not have a high priest who is unable to sympathize with our weaknesses, but one who in every respect has been tempted as we are, yet without sin.”

III.  The Response of the Believer
