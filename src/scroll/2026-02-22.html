Welcome

We Are Glad You Are Here!

We are honored that you decided to join us this morning. Whether it is your first time in church, your first time in a long while, or part of your regular rhythm, we hope you will be encouraged by the fellowship with each other, and with our Lord, as we worship him together.

If this is your first time with us, please take a moment and fill out a Next Steps Card found on the back of the pew. We would love to meet you and help you get connected.

Children & Nursery

Children’s Church — Children ages 4 through 5th grade are welcome in the Fellowship Hall, located at the northeast corner of the church campus. Nursery and toddler rooms for infants through age 3 are in the Education Wing, with the entrance behind the playground on the west side of campus. Children are always welcome in worship!

Our Vision & Mission

Our vision is to see lives transformed by the power of the gospel, rooted in God’s Word and the Reformed tradition, in order to create a culture of discipleship at FPC where disciples WORSHIP holistically, GROW relationally in Christ, and SERVE boldly in the world for the glory of God. Our mission is to accomplish this by “Being Disciples and Making Disciples.”

Sermon Notes

“The Glue That Holds God’s People Together”

Luke 6:27–36 • Bobby Capps, Dir. of Outreach & Care

On this First Sunday of Lent, we turn our hearts toward the love that binds God’s people together — a love that is not drawn from the shallow well of human affection, but from the deep and steadfast mercy of our Father. Jesus calls us to love our enemies, to do good to those who hate us, and to show mercy just as our Father is merciful. This Lenten season, as we consider our own weakness and Christ’s faithfulness, may we be shaped by a love that looks like His.

I.  The Call to Love

Luke 6:27–28 — “But I say to you who hear, Love your enemies, do good to those who hate you, bless those who curse you, pray for those who abuse you.”

II.  The Character of Love

Luke 6:31 — “And as you wish that others would do to you, do so to them.”

1 John 4:19 — “We love because he first loved us.”

III.  The Reward of Love

Luke 6:35–36 — “Your reward will be great, and you will be sons of the Most High… Be merciful, even as your Father is merciful.”

1 John 4:19 — “We love because he first loved us.”
