Welcome

We Are Glad You Are Here!

We are honored that you decided to join us this morning. Whether it is your first time in church, your first time in a long while, or part of your regular rhythm, we hope you will be encouraged by the fellowship with each other, and with our Lord, as we worship him together.

If this is your first time with us, please take a moment and fill out a Next Steps Card found on the back of the pew. We would love to meet you and help you get connected.

Children & Nursery

Children’s Church — Children ages 4 through 5th grade are welcome in the Fellowship Hall, located at the northeast corner of the church campus. Nursery rooms for infants through age 3 are in the Education Wing, with the entrance behind the playground on the west side of campus. Children are always welcome in worship!

Our Vision & Mission

Our vision is to see lives transformed by the power of the gospel, rooted in God’s Word and the Reformed tradition, in order to create a culture of discipleship at FPC where disciples WORSHIP holistically, GROW relationally in Christ, and SERVE boldly in the world for the glory of God. Our mission is to accomplish this by “Being Disciples and Making Disciples.”

Infant Baptism We believe infant baptism is a sign and seal of God’s covenant of grace, marking the children of believers as members of the visible church just as circumcision did under the old covenant. It does not guarantee salvation, but it signifies God’s promises and calls the child to personal faith and repentance as they grow. (Gen. 17:7–12; Col. 2:11–12)

Sermon Outline

“Praying as Christ Taught Us: Soli Deo Gloria”

Romans 11:33–36 • Rev. Waring Porter

In this sermon series, we have learned that when Jesus teaches us to pray in Matthew 6, He doesn’t start with our needs—He starts with God: “Our Father in heaven, hallowed be your name.” Before prayer ever changes our circumstances, it is meant to reorient our hearts toward the glory of God. As we come to the end of the Lord’s Prayer, we see where all of this has been heading. After eleven chapters of deep theology in his letter to the church in Rome, the apostle Paul doesn’t conclude with a neat summary—he explodes in worship. The final word of prayer is praise, and the reason is simple: all true prayer ends where it began—with the glory of God alone.

I.  The Goal of Prayer (vv. 33–34)

Romans 11:33 — “Oh, the depth of the riches and wisdom and knowledge of God! How unsearchable are his judgments and how inscrutable his ways!”

II.  The Delight of Prayer (v. 36)

Romans 11:36a — “For from him and through him and to him are all things.”

III.  The Hope of Prayer (v. 36b)

Romans 11:36b — “To him be glory forever. Amen.”

The final word of prayer is praise—because the final reality of all things is the glory of God.

Resources: Calvin’s Commentaries (Vol. IX), John Calvin; The Lord’s Prayer, Kevin DeYoung; Romans, William Hendrickson; Matthew Henry’s Commentary (Vol. 6); Lead With Prayer, Doolittle, Greer, Skoog; Praying the Lord’s Prayer, J.I. Packer; The Lord’s Prayer, Thomas Watson
