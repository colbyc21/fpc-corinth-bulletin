Our Vision & Mission

Our vision is to see lives transformed by the power of the gospel, rooted in God’s Word and the Reformed tradition, in order to create a culture of discipleship at FPC where disciples WORSHIP holistically, GROW relationally in Christ, and SERVE boldly in the world for the glory of God. Our mission is to accomplish this by “Being Disciples and Making Disciples.”

Call to Worship We are summoned to worship by God’s word, calling His people into His presence to honor Him in spirit and truth, opening a sacred conversation that unfolds throughout the service. *The Hymn:  #262 “Holy, Holy, Holy” (vv. 1–3)

“Holy, Holy, Holy” Written by Reginald Heber for Trinity Sunday, this majestic hymn grounds our opening liturgy in the absolute glory, sovereignty, and transcendence of God. The lyrics echo the eternal heavenly worship of Revelation 4:8 and Isaiah 6:3. Set to John B. Dykes’ powerful tune NICAEA, it calls us to join the saints and angels in praising the triune God—the ultimate focus of our corporate worship.

The Lord’s Supper

The Sacrament of The Lord’s Supper

Invitation to the Table

The Lord’s Supper The Lord’s Supper is a means of grace in which Christ spiritually nourishes believers, strengthening their faith as they partake of the bread and cup in union with Him. We reflect on how thankful we are for the work of Christ on our behalf, and we pray that his grace would work more powerfully in our lives to change us more into his likeness. The sacrament is a tangible, concrete gift where we can physically remember the goodness of the gospel story.

Corporate Prayer of Confession

Merciful Father, You alone are worthy of our worship, yet we confess that we have often sought security in ourselves rather than in You. We trust our own wisdom, rely on our own strength, and pursue our own priorities before seeking Your kingdom. Forgive us for loving the gifts more than the Giver and for allowing the cares of this world to distract us from wholehearted devotion to Christ. Restore our hearts, we pray, that we may worship You with sincerity, trust You with confidence, and delight in walking in Your ways. As we come to Your Table, remind us that our hope is not found in what we have done, but in what Christ has done for us. By Your Spirit, grant us true repentance, renewed faith, and grateful hearts as we receive the signs and seals of Your covenant grace.

Silent Confession

Prayer of Confession Our worship moves from adoration to grace, to confession, then assurance, and finally to the Table. This prayer intentionally acknowledges our tendency toward self-reliance while anticipating the grace that Christ offers in the Sacrament.

Sermon Outline

“Restored by Grace: First Things First”

Rev. Waring Porter  ·  Ezra 3:1–7

Ezra reminds us that the greatest need of God’s covenant people was not earthly security but renewed fellowship with the living God. Before they could rebuild a city, they had to seek the God who dwelt in their midst. That is still God’s order today. Before His people need protection from their enemies, they need peace with their God. God calls His people to Himself. Worship is not merely one activity among many — it is the center from which every other part of life receives its direction and meaning. The Israelites’ greatest problem has never been Babylon. It has never been Persia. It has never been hostile neighbors. Their greatest problem has always been sin. That is still true today. Our greatest need is reconciliation with God.

“Ascribe to the Lord the glory due his name.” — Psalm 29:2
