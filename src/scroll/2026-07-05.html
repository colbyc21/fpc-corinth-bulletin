Welcome

We Are Glad You Are Here!

We are honored that you decided to join us this morning. Whether it is your first time in church, your first time in a long while, or part of your regular rhythm, we hope you will be encouraged by the fellowship with each other, and with our Lord, as we worship him together.

If this is your first time with us, please take a moment and fill out a Next Steps Card found on the back of the pew. We would love to meet you and help you get connected.

Children & Nursery

Children’s Church — The Youth Group is leading Children’s Church this summer. Nursery rooms for infants through age 3 are in the Education Wing, with the entrance behind the playground on the west side of campus. Children are always welcome in worship!

Our Vision & Mission

Our vision is to see lives transformed by the power of the gospel, rooted in God’s Word and the Reformed tradition, in order to create a culture of discipleship at FPC where disciples WORSHIP holistically, GROW relationally in Christ, and SERVE boldly in the world for the glory of God. Our mission is to accomplish this by “Being Disciples and Making Disciples.”

Corporate Prayer of Confession

Almighty Creator God, who by Your Son has given us access to Your throne of grace and who has made wondrous promises to us of things eternal, we rightly owe You our total, unqualified worship and obedience. Yet we have foolishly and willfully strayed like lost sheep by evil things we have done and by good things we have left undone. We deserve nothing but Your wrath and displeasure, but, because of Your covenantal faithfulness and fatherly love, we are bold to ask for Your complete forgiveness of all the sins we have committed. Grant to us the certain knowledge of our full pardon, and move us to constant, grateful obedience, that Your wonderful name may be glorified now and forever. It is in the name of Jesus, our Redeemer, we pray.

With her torch raised to the sky;
And all who see her, know she stands
For liberty for you and me.

I’m so proud to be called an American,

I will honor our flag and our trust in God

On lonely Golgotha, stood a cross
With my Lord raised to the sky.
And all who kneel there, live forever
As all the saved can testify!
And I’m so glad to be called a Christian,
To be named with the ransomed and whole!
As the Statue liberates the citizen,
So the cross liberates the soul.

Oh, the cross is my Statue of Liberty;

Unashamed, I’ll proclaim that a rugged cross
Is my Statue of Liberty! My Liberty!
