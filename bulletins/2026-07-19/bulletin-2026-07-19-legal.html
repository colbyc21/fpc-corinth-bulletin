Our Vision & Mission

Our vision is to see lives transformed by the power of the gospel, rooted in God’s Word and the Reformed tradition, in order to create a culture of discipleship at FPC where disciples WORSHIP holistically, GROW relationally in Christ, and SERVE boldly in the world for the glory of God. Our mission is to accomplish this by “Being Disciples and Making Disciples.”

Sermon Outline

“Restored by Grace: Tears and Triumph”

Rev. Waring Porter  ·  Ezra 3:8–13

God’s people have returned from seventy years of exile. The altar has been rebuilt, sacrifices have resumed, and now the foundation of the Temple is finally being laid. This should be a day of pure celebration. Instead, Ezra tells us something surprising. Some people shout for joy. Others weep aloud. The sounds become so intertwined that no one standing at a distance can tell one from the other. Perhaps you’ve experienced moments like that. Life with God often refuses to fit into simple emotional categories. This passage reminds us that God’s restoring work often produces grief over what has been lost and hope for what He has promised. Those emotions are not enemies. In fact, they often exist together in the hearts of God’s people.

Our Father, who art in heaven, hallowed be thy Name. Thy Kingdom come, thy will be done on earth, as it is in heaven. Give us this day our daily bread, and forgive us our debts, as we forgive our debtors. Lead us not into temptation, but deliver us from evil. Invocation The word “invocation” suggests that the congregation calls upon God, yet this must not be misunderstood to mean that we bring God into our presence or that His nearness depends on our request. The Lord is already present with His people before our worship begins. To “invoke” is to call upon or earnestly seek. So, we lift our voices to God, asking Him to draw near and enable us to worship Him rightly for His character and mighty works.

Corporate Prayer of Confession

Merciful Father, we confess that we are too often discouraged by what we see rather than strengthened by what You have promised. We compare the present to the past, trust our own judgment more than Your Word, and forget that You are faithfully accomplishing Your purposes in Your perfect time. Forgive us for doubting Your presence and for failing to rest in Your covenant promises. Renew our faith by Your Holy Spirit, that we may rejoice in Your work, serve You with steadfast hearts, and fix our hope on Christ, whose glory far surpasses all earthly glory. We ask this in His holy name.

Infant Baptism We believe infant baptism is a sign and seal of God’s covenant of grace, marking the children of believers as members of the visible church just as circumcision did under the old covenant. It does not guarantee salvation, but it signifies God’s promises and calls the child to personal faith and repentance as they grow. (Gen. 17:7–12; Col. 2:11–12)
