Welcome

We Are Glad You Are Here!

We are honored that you decided to join us this morning. Whether it is your first time in church, your first time in a long while, or part of your regular rhythm, we hope you will be encouraged by the fellowship with each other, and with our Lord, as we worship him together.

If this is your first time with us, please take a moment and fill out a Next Steps Card found on the back of the pew. We would love to meet you and help you get connected.

Children & Nursery

Children’s Church — The Youth Group is leading Children’s Church during the month of June. Nursery rooms for infants through age 3 are in the Education Wing, with the entrance behind the playground on the west side of campus. Children are always welcome in worship!

Our Vision & Mission

Our vision is to see lives transformed by the power of the gospel, rooted in God’s Word and the Reformed tradition, in order to create a culture of discipleship at FPC where disciples WORSHIP holistically, GROW relationally in Christ, and SERVE boldly in the world for the glory of God. Our mission is to accomplish this by “Being Disciples and Making Disciples.”

Sermon Outline

“Restored by Grace: The God Who Stirs Hearts”

Rev. Waring Porter  ·  Ezra 1:1–11

Ezra tells the story of the restoration of God’s people — a people returning home, a temple rebuilt, worship renewed, and hearts called back to covenant faithfulness. Beneath it all runs a deeper theme: God’s gracious work of restoring what sin had broken. Restoration does not begin with human strength; it begins with divine grace. As we journey through Ezra together, we will discover again that God’s people are restored by grace — grace that pursues, preserves, rebuilds, and renews.

I. God Sovereignly Rules Over the Nations (vv. 1–4)

“Who says of Cyrus, ‘He is my shepherd, and he shall fulfill all my purpose.’” — Isa. 44:28

“I call you by your name… though you do not know me.” — Isa. 45:4

II. God Faithfully Keeps His Covenant Promises (vv. 1, 5–6)

“When seventy years are completed for Babylon… I will fulfill to you my promise and bring you back.” — Jer. 29:10

III. God Leads His People into a Greater Exodus Through Christ (vv. 7–11)

Corporate Prayer of Confession

Holy and Sovereign God, we confess that we do not fully trust your rule. You command the universe, yet we live in anxiety. You hold our breath, yet we rely on our own strength. We doubt your good providence when trials come. We look at a broken world and fear you have lost control. Forgive our stubborn unbelief. Forgive our desire to sit on your throne. Calm our fears with the truth of your supremacy. Remind us that nothing can thwart your holy decrees. Increase our faith in your majestic, loving governance. We ask this through Jesus Christ, our reigning Lord.
